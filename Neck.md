## CT

### Normal

**Technique:**

Plain and IV contrast enhanced helical CT images were obtained from the skull base to the thoracic inlet. Soft tissue and bone windows were reconstructed.

**Findings:**

There is good definition of the compartmental anatomy of the neck with no evidence of a mass lesion.

The pharyngeal and glottic regions are normal. The parapharyngeal space is normal. The tonsils and adenoids are also normal. The nasal pyriform apertures are unremarkable.
The true and false vocal cords, pyriform sinus, laryngeal ventricle and laryngeal cartilages are normal.

The parotid, submandibular and thyroid glands are normal.

The muscles and tendons of the neck are  normal.

There is no cervical lymphadenopathy. The lung apices are clear of opacities.

There is no bony abnormality. The craniocervical junction and spinal canal are free of mass lesions.

The common carotid arteries, carotid bulbs, internal carotid and external carotid arteries have a normal appearance. There is no evidence of an atheroma, stenosis, aneurysm or dissection.

The jugular veins are normal.

**Conclusion:**

Normal CT neck.
