## CT ANGIOGRAPHY OF THE LOWER LIMBS
Indication:  Severe peripheral arterial disease
Technique: Pre- and post-contrast 0.5mm axial slices were obtained from the lower aortic bifurcation at L4 to the feet with 3D volume rendering and MIP reconstructions.   
Findings: 
There are widespread calcified atheromatous plaques in all the arteries of both lower limbs, bilateral common iliac artery, aortic bifurcation and distal abdominal aorta.
There are multiple haemodynamically significant stenoses in the bilateral anterior and posterior tibial arteries with the right anterior tibial artery worst affected.
There is near-total occlusion of the middle two-thirds of the right anterior tibial artery with distal reconstitution of the distal third arterial segment. 
Less severe stenoses are noted in the other arteries of both legs.
There is no thrombus, aneurysm or dissection in any of the vessels
Marked soft tissue irregularity, fat stranding and ulceration is noted in the right foot and distal third of the right leg.
Conclusion: Severe peripheral arterial disease
